About

I am a PhD candidate in Epidemiology at the University of Pittsburgh, focusing on Aging Epidemiology under the mentorship of Dr. Elsa S. Strotmeyer .

My research lives at the intersection of obesity and healthy aging. I study body composition phenotypes and how they shape geriatric outcomes such as falls and functional decline—because not all obesity (or aging) looks the same.

I am particularly drawn to advanced statistical analysis and modeling using large-scale, longitudinal population-based data. Much of my work involves data harmonization, risk factor identification, and prediction modeling across multiple aging cohorts. I primarily work in R and SAS, and I am actively expanding my toolkit into ML-based prediction modeling using Python.

Research Interests

  • Obesity phenotyping and multimodal evaluation of body composition
  • Large-scale health data analysis / cross-cohort harmonization
  • Functional safety and risk prediction modeling for mobility-related outcomes in aging populations
